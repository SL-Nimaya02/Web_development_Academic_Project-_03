ASTROGEOLOGY: WHAT IS IT ?

The geology of the planets, their moons, asteroids, and other solid objects in the solar system is the subject of astrogeology. With the aid of this research, scientists are better able to comprehend how the evolution of the Earth compares to that of its solar system neighbors.

The USGS Astrogeology Science Center

Research on the interactions of planetary surfaces and surrounding atmospheres is done at the USGS Astrogeology Science Center. Particularly, there is a transfer of momentum (from the wind), vapor (from evaporation/sublimation), liquid (from rainfall, percolation, and infiltration), and solid (from snow) between surfaces and atmospheres.

IMPORTANT OF ASTROGEOLOGY

The geology of the planets, their moons, asteroids, and other solid objects in the solar system is the subject of astrogeology. With the aid of this research, scientists are better able to comprehend how the evolution of the Earth compares to that of its solar system neighbors.

ASTROGEOLOGY APPLICATIONS

Geologic factors must be taken into account when deciding where to place piers for bridges, dams, buildings, roads, and other civil features. Geologists and geophysicists have led the exploration for fossil fuels and geothermal energy, as well as locating commercially valuable minerals.
